Negative Stiffness Brace (NSB)

The NSB (Herkal & Nagarajaiah, J. Struct. Eng. 2025) is the next-generation device: installed exactly like a conventional brace, with pre-compressed springs, gap-spring assemblies (GSA), and links in series — achieving high force magnification with much lower spring stiffness requirements than the original NSD.

Key Advantage

Why NSD/NSB + Viscous Damper Works

✦ Geometric Nonlinearity — Adaptive Stiffness

The NSD/NSB harnesses geometric nonlinearity to adapt the structural stiffness without damage to the main structure. By engaging negative stiffness, the combined system effectively weakens the structure in a controlled, fully elastic manner — eliminating inelastic excursions and permanent deformation.

✦ Damping Amplification — 5× to 6×

When paired with a viscous damper, the NSD/NSB increases the effective damping by 5 to 6 times that of the viscous damper acting alone — a dramatic amplification achieved passively, with no power or active control required.

✦ Dual Structural Benefits

Reduced drift: Inter-story drifts are significantly curtailed across all stories, protecting both structural and non-structural components.
Reduced base force: The force transmitted through the structure to the foundation is substantially lowered, protecting the entire load path.

✦ Universal Applicability — Fixed-Base & Base-Isolated

This new method protects structures whether in the fixed-base or base-isolated condition, enabling adaptivity while remaining fully passive. It significantly enhances energy dissipation while limiting damage to the primary structure — a unified solution across structural configurations. Method and invention validated by extensive component and shake table testing in buildings and bridges.

■ Fixed Base (large sway)   ■ Base Isolated (base moves, tower steady)   ■ Fixed Base + NSBs (minimal drift)

✦ Key Experimental Validation

Extensive shake table testing at UB validated the method proposed that NSD/NSB + damper placed at the lowest story of a MDOF structure reduces acceleration, inter-story drifts, and base shear in all stories, with limited inelastic excursions or permanent deformation in the main structural frame. The shake table validation was equally effective and showed the non-resonant protection in the bridge structure due to the NSD and hysteretic (friction-type) damping under strong earthquakes.

Applications & Impact

Structural Systems Protected

01
SDOF & Fixed-Base Buildings
Foundation of the research. NSD + damper in a single story eliminates inelastic excursions while dramatically cutting base shear and acceleration (Pasala et al. 2013, 2014, 2015).
02
Seismically Isolated Structures
NSD added to isolation systems reduces isolator displacement demand while retaining the drift & acceleration benefits. Shake table validated (Sarlis et al. 2016).
03
Highway Bridges
RPI shake table tests confirmed NSD seismic protection for bridge structures under multi-directional ground motions (Attary et al. 2015).
04
Tall Buildings — Wind & EQ
Negative Stiffness Damped Outrigger (NSDO) system and inerter-NSD combos for multi-hazard high-rise protection (Nagarajaiah et al. 2022; Wang & Nagarajaiah 2023).
05
TMD Retrofit & Vibration Isolation
NSDs correct detuning of existing TMDs; also reduce transmissibility in isolation systems without added mass (Nagarajaiah, Zou, Herkal 2022).
18+ years of continuous innovation: NSF-funded 2008 → NSD concept → next-gen NSB brace device.
